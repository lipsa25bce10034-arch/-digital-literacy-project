# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking details.
A typical case involves a student receiving a message or phone call claiming that they have won a reward or need to receive money. The fraudster then sends a payment request or asks the victim to scan a QR code. The victim, thinking they will receive money, approves the request or scans the code, which actually results in money being debited from their account.
Sometimes, fraudsters also pretend to be bank officials and ask for OTPs or PINs. Once they get access, they quickly transfer money from the victim’s account.
Students are often targeted because they use digital payments frequently and may not be aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal information.
This type of cybercrime is increasing due to the rapid growth of digital transactions. Awareness and careful handling of payment requests are essential to prevent such frauds.
